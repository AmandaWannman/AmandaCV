About

From the moment I learned how to scuba dive, took my first breath under water and discovered a whole new world, I was hooked. At the same time I could see the problems around me. As a marine biologist I hope to participate in relieving some of these issues and create a more sustainabile global climate. I spent the better part of my master’s years studying tropical ecology and conservation in Jamaica and Ecuador. My thesis work was using Photo-ID to track spatial distribution of the green sea turtle population in the Galapagos Islands. During my 3 months field work I tracked the migration patterns of some of the individuals in the archipelago. Through my work with sea turtle photo-ID I have used several different software such as PitMar and Internet of Turtles. My other passions are diving and teaching. My future vision is to be able to combine my love for the marine environment with education and community outreach though diving since I think seeing is believing.
